About the Project

Understanding the forest fire crisis and how AI helps protect our woodlands

The Forest Fire Crisis

Every year, millions of hectares of forest are destroyed by wildfires, devastating entire ecosystems:

  • Destruction of forest canopy, undergrowth & old-growth trees
  • Loss of wildlife habitats — animals, birds & insects displaced or killed
  • Soil erosion & watershed damage after forest cover is burned
  • Release of stored carbon, accelerating climate change
  • Threat to forest-dependent indigenous communities

Why Early Detection Matters

Most forest fires start small — a single spark in dry undergrowth. Early recognition saves entire ecosystems.

  • Forest rangers can respond in minutes instead of hours
  • Preserves tree canopy, root systems & forest floor biodiversity
  • Protects wildlife corridors & nesting habitats
  • Prevents fire from spreading to neighboring forest zones
  • Enables evacuation of forest workers & nearby communities

How ML Recognizes Forest Fire

CNN (InceptionV3)
Primary Model

Deep CNN pre-trained on ImageNet, fine-tuned on 32,000+ real forest fire, smoke & woodland images. Achieves 86.7% accuracy across three classes.

Color Analysis (HSV)
Hybrid Layer

HSV color-space analysis detects fire-orange & smoke-grey tones typical of forest burn areas. Provides secondary verification.

Hybrid Decision Engine
Fusion

Combines CNN confidence (90% weight) with color analysis evidence to make robust forest fire recognition decisions.
